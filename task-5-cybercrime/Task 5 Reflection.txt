Task 5 Reflection: Cybercrime Awareness

While researching UPI fraud for Task 5, the thing that surprised me the most was
how heavily scammers rely on psychological manipulation rather than advanced
hacking. They don't break into your phone; they simply rush you into making a
mistake, like entering a UPI PIN under the false belief that it is required to receive
money. As a college student who constantly uses UPI for everything from splitting
canteen bills to paying for daily expenses, this was an eye-opener. As a result of
this research, one habit I will permanently change is how I handle transaction
requests. I will no longer approve payment requests or scan QR codes while
distracted or on a phone call. I plan to slow down, read the screen prompts
carefully, and strictly remember that a PIN is never needed to receive funds.
