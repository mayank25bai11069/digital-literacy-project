Task 4 Reflection: Digital Communication

One major problem I have noticed in digital communication is how easily tone can
be misinterpreted in text-based messages. For example, during a recent college
hackathon, quick, fragmented messages sent in our team group chat came across
as overly blunt, leading to a brief moment of unnecessary friction. Because we
were rushing to build our project, we lost the polite framing that usually softens
face-to-face feedback. This experience highlighted why taking the time to write
well-structured, professional emails is crucial when making requests to professors
or recruiters. Recognizing the limits of instant messaging and actively choosing the
right platform and tone is essential for effective digital collaboration.
